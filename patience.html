Patience

The Art of Patience

Patience is defined as the the ability to endure any sort of trial without being overcome with emotion.

As we grow in patience we are able to accept God and His timing in all things. Our ability to grow in patience comes with growth in our ability to manage our expectations. (see Alma 17:10-11)

It is commanded of us to be patient with everyone that we come in contact with. Although it may be harder to show patience to people that do not respect you. But our faith will help us overcome these difficulties as it has been shown in the Book of Mormon. Mosiah 24: 9-16

“Life is full of difficulties, some minor and others of a more serious nature. There seems to be an unending supply of challenges for one and all. Our problem is that we often expect instantaneous solutions to such challenges, forgetting that frequently the heavenly virtue of patience is required.” (President Thomas S. Monson, “Patience - a Heavenly Virture” Ensign, November 1995).
