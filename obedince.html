Obedience

Obedience is defined as the willingness to submit to God’s will and keep His commandments out of love for Him.

Jesus Christ set the perfect example of obedience, declaring, “I came down from heaven, not to do mine own will, but the will of him that sent me” (John 6:38). His entire mortal life demonstrated His commitment to do His Father’s will. He taught us that obedience is the pathway to blessings and salvation. (see John 14:15)

In the Book of Mormon, King Benjamin’s people showed their commitment to obedience by covenanting to keep God’s commandments, which brought them closer to Christ. We too are commanded to live by every word that comes from God, knowing that blessings come through obedience to eternal laws. (see Mosiah 5:1-7 and Doctrine and Covenants 130:20-21)

The ultimate example of obedience is found in the Savior’s sacrifice in the Garden of Gethsemane, where He prayed, “Not my will, but thine, be done” (Luke 22:42). Through His complete submission, He made salvation possible for all God’s children. We follow His example when we obey God out of love and with a willing heart. (see Mosiah 15:7)

“When the Lord commands, do it.” (Prophet Joseph Smith, Doctrine and Covenants 42:78).
