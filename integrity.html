Integrity

Learn about Integrity

Integrity is defined as living in harmony with moral principles and being honest in all that you do. It means being true to who you are, to your covenants with God, and to your values, even when no one is watching.

Living with integrity means consistently choosing what is right, even when it is difficult. It requires being honest in your words and actions, honoring commitments, and standing firm in your principles. Those who live with integrity earn the trust of others and show their commitment to God. The stripling warriors in the Book of Mormon exemplified integrity when they remained true to their covenants and their faith despite immense challenges. (see Alma 53:20-21)

Integrity is essential in a world full of distractions and temptations. It helps us resist deceit, hypocrisy, and the influence of worldly pressures. The Prophet Joseph Smith taught the importance of being true to God, stating, "I made this my rule: When the Lord commands, do it." (see Doctrine and Covenants 42:78)

When we live with integrity, we reflect the character of Jesus Christ, who lived a perfect life and was unwavering in His obedience and honesty. Integrity gives us confidence to face God without shame, knowing that we have done our best to align our lives with His teachings. As the Psalmist wrote, “He that walketh uprightly, and worketh righteousness, and speaketh the truth in his heart” will dwell in the Lord’s holy hill. (see Psalm 15:1-2)

“A person of integrity can be trusted, relied upon, and counted on to do what is right. Integrity is the foundation of character and the bedrock of trust.” (President Gordon B. Hinckley, "Who Are You?" General Conference, April 1990)

Integrity also helps us fulfill our divine potential. It enables us to be trustworthy leaders, reliable friends, and faithful disciples of Jesus Christ. As we live with integrity, we gain strength, peace, and confidence to navigate life’s challenges, knowing that we are living as God desires.
