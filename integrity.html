Integrity

Learn about Integrity

Hope is more than wishful thinking. It is living our lives with purpose grounded in our faith. Through scripture we learn that we can have the confidence in good things to come. (see Hebrews 9:11).

What is the basis of our Hope? I ask this same question. In Moroni 7:41 we see that our hope must be based in Jesus Christ. His life, crucifixion and resurrection allow us to believe and hope for life beyond this moment on earth. Not only this but it can anchor your life and your heart. As we have something to believe in we can feel supported and at peace despite challenges and difficulties. (see Ether 12:4)

Our commitment to living the Gospel of Jesus Christ will in turn increase our ability to grow in hope. For example, as we repent and feel the forgiveness that we receive from Jesus Christ we can find a new found hope that Christ loves us and is looking out for us. (see 2 Nephi 31:20)

Here is a quote from the April 2024 General Conference By Elder Jose L. Alonso about hope:

“When we encounter difficulties, we naturally tend to concentrate on the obstacles we face. Our challenges are tangible and command our attention, yet the principle of surmounting them is in our focus. By placing Christ at the core of our thoughts and deeds, we align ourselves with His outlook and strength. This adjustment does not discount our struggles; instead, it helps us to navigate through them under divine guidance. As a result, we discover solutions and support that arise from a higher wisdom. Adopting this Christ-centric perspective empowers us with the fortitude and insight to turn our trials into victories, reminding us that with the Savior, what seems like a major problem can become a pathway to greater spiritual progress.” (Elder Jose L. Alonso, “Jesus Christ at the Center of Our Lives” General Conference, April 2024).
