Charity and Love

What is Christlike charity and love?

Charity is defined in the scriptures as the pure love of Christ (see Moroni 7:47). Therefore it is closely related to the attribute of love. It is the most important commandment as we have learned from Jesus Christ Himself (see Matthew 22:36-39).

Charity and love are characteristics that encourage action. As we looks to serve others our ability to love others will grow. Not only will our personal ability to show love but God bestows charity upon us as we try to follow Jesus Christ (see Moroni 7:48).

What is a way the scriptures tell us to obtain charity? The most well known invitation was given in the Book of Mormon. states that we must pray with all the energy of our heart to be filled with the love of Christ.

For a deeper study of the aspect of charity study the teachings of Paul in the New Testament (see 1 Corinthians 13)

“Above all the attributes of godliness and perfection, charity is the one most devoutly to be desired. Charity is more than love, far more; it is everlasting love, perfect love, the pure love of Christ which endureth forever. It is love so centered in righteousness that the possessor has no aim or desire except for the eternal welfare of his own soul and for the souls of those around him.” (Elder Bruce R. McConkie)
