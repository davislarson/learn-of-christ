Virtue

What is virtue?

Virtue is an attribute related to our moral standards. If we are virtuous we will have a pattern of behaviors that is related to righteousness.

Gaining virtue begins with our personal thoughts and desires. The way we act when we are alone defines how we will act over time. (see Doctrine and Covenants 121:45)

We can fight unrighteous thoughts by filling our thoughts with things that are uplifting and good. It is much harder for sinful thoughts to enter our mind when we are busy thinking of other things. (see Psalms 24:4)

The Lord as commanded us to grow in virtue. Doctrine and Covenants 46:33 states "And ye must practice virtue and holiness before me continually. Even so. Amen."

“Be virtuous and pure, seek the companionship of the Holy Ghost, bury your sins and transgressions, maintain your focus, and don’t let the fog of moral pollution obscure your goals.” (Sister Elaine S. Dalton, “Now Is the Time to Arise and Shine!” General Conference, April 2012).
