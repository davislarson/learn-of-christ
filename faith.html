Faith

Faith in Jesus Christ

Faith in Jesus Christ is the foundation for our salvation. The most important par of faith is that it must be centered on Jesus Christ as the Only Begotten Son of God and our Savior. Faith means trusting that Christ’s Atonement can bring forgiveness through repentance and that His power can sanctify us through the Holy Ghost. It’s not about having perfect knowledge but rather trusting in spiritual truths that we cannot yet see (see Ether 12:6).

Faith is more than belief, it's demonstrated through action (see Alma 32) These actions include following Christ’s teachings, serving others, choosing righteousness, and continually repenting and loving others. Faith leads to God's power in our lives, allows us to experience miracles according to His will (see Hebrews 11).

To grow in faith, you must come to know Christ more deeply by praying, studying the scriptures, and obeying His commandments. Doubt and sin can weaken faith, but consistent spiritual practices strengthen it.

Check out this quote from Neil L. Anderson from the Quorum of twelve apostles about faith

“Faith is not only a feeling; it is a decision. With prayer, study, obedience, and covenants, we build and fortify our faith. Our conviction of the Savior and His latter-day work becomes the powerful lens through which we judge all else. Then, as we find ourselves in the crucible of life, … we have the strength to take the right course” (Neil L. Andersen, “It’s True, Isn’t It? Then What Else Matters?” Ensign or Liahona, May 2007, 74).
