Diligence

Diligence is defined as steady, consistent, earnest, and energetic effort in doing the Lord’s work.

As we grow in diligence, we learn to work persistently and with great care, striving to serve effectively and efficiently. Diligence in the Lord’s work is a reflection of our love for Him and brings joy and satisfaction as we fulfill His purposes. It involves doing good things of our own free will and continuing until we have done all we can, even when challenges arise. (see Doctrine and Covenants 58:27-28)

The Lord expects us to focus on what matters most, avoiding distractions and wasted time. Diligence requires prayer for guidance and strength, effective planning, and the determination to press forward in faith. Through this effort, we honor the Lord and grow in our ability to serve Him. (see 2 Nephi 31:20)

“Diligence in the Lord’s service is not only about what we do but about the spirit in which we do it. It requires patience, discipline, and a willingness to work for the things that truly matter.” (Elder Dieter F. Uchtdorf, “Your Potential, Your Priority”, Ensign, May 2011).
